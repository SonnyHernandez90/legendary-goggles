What is Computer Vision?

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that allows computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world, such as images and videos. It helps machines "see" and make decisions based on what they observe.

How It Works

Computer vision systems process digital images by identifying patterns, shapes, colors, and objects. These systems often use machine learning and deep learning models to recognize important features and make predictions.

Common Applications

  • Facial recognition
  • Self-driving cars
  • Medical image analysis
  • Security and surveillance
  • Object detection in manufacturing
  • Augmented reality

Why It Matters

Computer vision is important because it helps automate tasks that normally require human sight. This can improve accuracy, save time, and make systems smarter in many industries such as healthcare, robotics, transportation, and engineering.
